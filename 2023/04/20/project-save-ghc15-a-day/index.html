Project Save Ghc15 A Day

Sianokie2 months ago3 hours ago011 mins

Earlier this week GHC15 trended. What started as a mere suggestion on saving tips to help guys give their girlfriends befitting birthday presents turned out to have infuriated the guys instead. Reading some of the comments, I wondered if some of these guys had girlfriends. It’ll be disheartening to see your boyfriend’s tweet suggesting that women don’t deserve all that and more. Agreed, Ghc15 a day summing up to Ghc5,475 a year may seem a bit much to spend in one day especially if you don’t have that much to spare but maybe, just maybe this whole thing has been taken out of context. I think all she was trying to say is, put a bit more effort into gifting. Knowing birthdays are an annual thing, it’ll be wise to save towards them just like we save towards every other thing. It’s an investment into your relationship. 

I took the discussion to some male friends and I was a bit sad if not angry. Listening to them speak, I realized that perhaps a lot of girls have been forced to accept that they don’t like gifts and all the flashy things that make birthdays special. Guys live under the illusion that girls who expect less or nothing are good girlfriends and for that matter, good marriage material. Let me clear this up for you guys, girls love gifts regardless of what they tell you. No girlfriend is going to get angry because her boyfriend did something nice for her. Even when we say we want a lowkey birthday this year,  we still want you to put in effort. Remember when they said women never say what they mean? Yeah, this is one instance.

God bless the guys who move heaven and earth to put smiles on their girlfriend’s faces on special occasions like birthdays and Valentine’s day. I don’t think it will hurt to remind your girlfriend on 1 day out of the 365 days that she still means a lot to you. The guys who also say they are fine surprising their girls on regular days but not on special occasions, I don’t understand you. You’re bae on regular days and on days when it actually matters, you disappear. In what world does that make sense?

Don’t get me wrong, I’m not equating material things to love but please, and please again, stop comparing current birthday celebrations to our parents’ times. Times have changed and love may have taken on a different meaning. Expression of love has changed. Get used to it.

I’m a stickler for (relatively) grand gestures on birthdays and Valentine’s day. I love gifts and I don’t even hide it. Before you crucify me, that’s how I was brought up by the guys I’ve been with and even some male friends. I wasn’t given a house, a car, or anything but I am used to receiving routine thoughtful gifts that make me feel loved. To think that some girls never experience this, not even on their birthdays is heartbreaking. Some guys mask their lack of effort, thoughtfulness, and capability with statements like we have a mutual understanding or my girlfriend doesn’t like gifts.

Before you conclude your girl doesn’t like the gifts, attention, and affection, ask yourself if you had anything to do with her decision. Ask yourself if you ever said something that may have caused her to hide her true self. Even if she said she doesn’t want anything too big on her birthday, has it occurred to you that some ex might have made her feel she deserved less and it’s up to you to prove to her that she deserves every time and money you spend on her.

Dear girls, before you smile and think this is your piece…

The guys also say they are tired of the scams we present as gifts. They are tired of boxer shorts, vests, socks, shirts, and even sex. Lol, what then do they want huh? Being a girl, I know how difficult it is to come up with gift ideas for guys. I mean, you can’t get it wrong with handbags, shoes, hair, make-up, clothes, cake, dinner, vacation, and a host of other things you can always choose from when getting gifts for girls.

However, when we get to guys, it’s difficult. What you know your boyfriend actually wants is expensive and beyond your budget. For the things you can afford, you risk being told by your boyfriend that you bought something fake. It’s hard for us but maybe we can consult other guys so we come up with things they will appreciate because believe it or not, guys like the hype and attention too.

I once was with a guy who said he’s never received any gift on his birthday before. He even asked me not to bother getting him anything. I did anyway, and seeing his eyes light up as he opened his present was worth every dime I spent. You don’t need to go broke spending on gifts, but put in effort. It turns out they like nice outings, shoes, tickets to events, paying for an online class, and even being posted on our statuses(not the type where he’s the only one viewing👿). When you’re on a tight budget, get creative. Some thrills are cheap…” less expensive” if you’re sensitive. Bottom line is, guys go through hell and spend a lot on our birthdays. It’ll be nice to reciprocate.

When it’s time to spend on our significant others and we can afford to, let’s kindly go the extra mile. Let’s try and suppress the thoughts about the worst-case scenarios where we think after all we’ve spent, these people will either leave us or cheat on us. It’s a very toxic way to think in a relationship. May we also not get to that point where we’ve been together so long we begin to disregard important days. Finally, when it comes to gifting in relationships, don’t take the recipient’s word for it. Always give your best and spoil them even if they don’t want to be. Too much is better than too little!

Being Single: A Guy’s Perspective.

Sianokie1 month ago14 hours ago010 mins

I said weekends are hard and they really are for recently single people. I had one of those weekends that send you 10 steps back on your healing progress. This particular one hurts because I feel I brought it on myself although it was a classic unintended consequence to a well-intended action. I should have been strong-willed and not have allowed myself to be taken for granted, objectified even!  My head’s messed up and I even hate myself a little bit. I bet I’m not making sense, but I can’t bring myself to talk about it. It’s all on me so I won’t waste your time ranting about it. All I can say is, sometimes the people we love just love themselves more.

Anyway, being single is hard and I was wondering how it feels for men. Do they go through all the emotional pain we do? Do they cry? Are there regrets? If I’m to judge from my ex’s experience, I’d say it’s a cruise for men. He has a way of being very convincing with his words but very aloof with his actions when it comes to our breakup and how he’s holding up. But that’s fine, guys never really deal with breakups, they just carry on with their lives. Well, today I want to share a friend’s journey and I really want to hear from other guys in the comments section. He’s been single for 5 years and It’s kinda making my 3 months look like a joke but pain is pain lol.  Anyway, read all about it below…

Hello Candid Viberz (Can I call you guys that? Lol)

It’s been almost 5 years since I had a special someone in my life. It almost feels like being single is normal. Do not get me wrong, I have enjoyed being single. It gives you a lot of time to get yourself together. But truth be told, no man is an island. I must say that in these past few years, a lot has gone both right and wrong. I have met amazing people, cut some people out of my life, gotten my spiritual life back on track and the list continues. However, one thing I have noticed is that there are those low moments you get that you just cannot seem to come out of, no matter how much you pray, talk to someone, or even distract yourself with other things. It is these moments that you should cherish the most because that is the only time your mind can see the clearest and think the sharpest.

They say, “All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy”, well this Jack has been dull for a very long time. Just to clarify, I am not trying to say that finding someone special would have made a difference these past years, however, I am not disputing that it could have.  What I am trying to say instead is that, being single or being alone is not all that bad, but if you let yourself believe that it is then you are only making it worse. Let’s talk about the social life, you know, going out and meeting new people. Yeah, I suck at that. It’s not even the going out part that is hard, it’s the meeting new people part. Funny thing is that I am a very social person, but only around the people I feel comfortable around. I am what they call an introverted extrovert. That’s not a thing by the way, but you get my point.

So back to the love stuff. Am I seeing anyone? Do I have someone my eyes are on? Do I plan to make any moves on anyone? Do I see marriage in my future and all that? Well, no, no, no, and yes. Just kidding, no, yes, no, and yes. I have spent a great deal of time following the whole social norm of the guy being the one to chase the girl to get the happy ending. I have a new concept, if I like you, I will do my best to let you know, but as to what happens after that is completely up to you. I’m tired of putting in so much effort and not getting the same energy back. It really drains your energy when things don’t go the way you would have hoped for. By that I mean when you start having those you know “she’s the one” thoughts only for you to end up being you and yourself back at square one.

So where do I stand on all this love stuff or dating stuff? To be very honest I do not know. It’s like one minute I want it and the next, I am just indifferent. I am not exactly the talkative type, but I know how to keep the conversation going if I want to or if I am enjoying it. Other than that, it takes quite a lot to get words out of me. It is my self-defense mechanism; it keeps me from spilling too much. I am very picky with whom I open up to because frankly these days, the only person you can ever trust is yourself, and God of course. Anyways I cannot say for sure I like the new me, but I do prefer the new me to the old me.

P.S.
A certain someone gave me the courage to write this, and I must say this person has been quite an amazing friend in the little time we have known each other. Some friends are for life and some are for a season, always choose what is best for you.

Regards,
Single Guy.

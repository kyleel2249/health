It\u2019s funny that I\u2019m writing about an ex today considering the fact that one of the million other reasons my ex left was because of his ex. He had feelings for her and felt guilty for how their relationship ended. Personally, I think guilt is a very strong feeling and can make you fall back in love with someone. It can make you overcompensate for things that are not even your fault. Well, I wish him well and if he went back to her, I hope he\u2019s happy\u2026or not. I really don\u2019t care. Now back to my ex.<\/span><\/p>\n

I ended up having more fun than I thought I would. It felt like old times. I hadn\u2019t seen him in a very long time. Occasionally, we would text to check up on each other. He\u2019d want to meet and I will come up with a reason why I can\u2019t. But today when he brought it up again I said why not. I wanted to know what the whole fuss about exes and sparking old flames is. Now I\u2019m glad I did. I needed to be out, I needed to be with someone familiar. I needed to be with someone who knew me. And oh, this one really knew me. He could decode all the hidden meanings in my words. He could tell what I needed even before I knew it myself. He made sure I got all the presents and attention I wanted. Why did we break up then? That\u2019s a story for another day.<\/p>\n

During our time together, he told me something that made me sadder than I should have been. He said one of the days when he suggested that we meet up and I declined, he came to my office to pick me up as a surprise. He wanted us to talk and see if we can work things out. But just as he was about to call me, I walked out of the building and sat in another man\u2019s car (My bf\u2019s at the time). It sounded like a movie, I thought he was joking until he described what I was wearing, the car, what I was holding, and what I did before sitting in the car. So he never spoke about it and never texted me. I was happy and he wasn\u2019t about to wreck that.\u00a0But I was single now and he wanted another shot.<\/p>\n

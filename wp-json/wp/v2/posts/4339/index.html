Movie nights are a great way to unwind and relax, but they can also be a calorie and unhealthy snack-filled event. Whether you’re trying to eat healthier or just want to mix things up, there are so many ways of making your movie night healthier without sacrificing flavor or fun. Here are some tips to help you make your next movie night a healthier experience.<\/span><\/p>\n

Choose healthier snacks:\u00a0<\/span><\/i><\/b>Instead of reaching for chips or candy, opt for healthier snack options. Popcorn is a classic movie snack, but instead of butter or caramel, try adding some flavor with nutritional yeast or spices like garlic powder and paprika. Fresh fruit and veggies, like sliced apples or carrot sticks, are also a great snack option.<\/span><\/p>\n

Make your own snacks: <\/span><\/i><\/b>Store-bought snacks are often high in calories and preservatives. Making your own snacks is a great way to control the ingredients and make sure they are healthy. Try making your own trail mix with nuts, dried fruit, and whole-grain cereal. Or whip up a batch of homemade granola bars.<\/span><\/p>\n

Skip the soda:\u00a0<\/span><\/i><\/b>Soda is high in sugar and calories, and it’s not a great choice for a healthy movie night. Instead, try making your own flavored water by adding slices of cucumber, lemon, or fresh herbs to a pitcher of water.<\/span><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Choose the right movie:\u00a0<\/span><\/i><\/b>Watching a movie that inspires healthy choices can help set the tone for a healthy movie night. Documentaries about health and wellness, like “Forks Over Knives” or “Fed Up,” can motivate you to make healthier choices.<\/span><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/div>\n

Get active during the movie:\u00a0<\/i><\/b><\/span><\/span>Watching a movie doesn’t have to mean sitting still for hours. Get up and
\nstretch during a commercial break or do a mini-workout during the movie. You can even turn your living room into a mini-gym by using resistance bands or dumbbells during the movie.<\/span><\/p>\n

Watch your portion sizes: <\/span><\/i><\/b>Portion sizes can quickly add up when you’re snacking during a movie. Try portioning out your snacks before the movie starts, so you don’t mindlessly munch through an entire bag of chips.<\/span><\/p>\n

Enjoy your snacks mindfully:\u00a0<\/span><\/i><\/b>Mindful eating can help you savor your food and enjoy your movie night snacks without overindulging. Instead of mindlessly munching through the entire movie, try focusing on the flavor and texture of each bite.<\/span><\/p>\n

Make it a group effort:\u00a0<\/span><\/i><\/b>Making movie night healthy can be more fun with a group. Get your friends or
\nfamily involved and make it a group effort. Have everyone bring a healthy snack or try a healthy recipe together.<\/span><\/p>\n

Choose a healthy movie-themed activity:\u00a0<\/span><\/i><\/b>If you want to take your healthy movie night to the next level, choose a movie-themed activity that promotes healthy habits. For example, if you’re watching a movie about surfing, try a yoga class that focuses on balance and strength.<\/span><\/p>\n
